Ethics Analysis

1. Identifying Ethical Risks

The newsletter subscription plugin collects various personal data fields such as name, email, occupation, income, spending habits, gender, and sexuality. The key ethical risks include:

  • Privacy violations due to collection of excessive or sensitive personal data.
  • Potential misuse of data through opaque profiling or manipulative targeting.
  • Algorithmic discrimination based on user behavior or device characteristics.
  • Psychological fatigue from overwhelming consent requests and personalization.
  • Default opt-ins that bypass informed user consent.
  • Security threats including identity theft from potential data breaches.

2. Ethical Standards from ACS Framework

According to the Australian Computer Society (ACS) Code of Ethics, the following principles must guide digital systems:

  • Privacy & Autonomy: Ensure users can make informed choices and retain control over their data.
  • Security & Trust: Limit data collection to what is necessary, and implement robust protection mechanisms.
  • Fairness & Inclusion: Avoid algorithmic bias and ensure accessibility for diverse user groups.
  • Transparency: Clearly disclose how user data is collected, processed, and stored.
  • Professional Responsibility: Never compromise user well-being or mental health for commercial advantage.

3. Proposed Solutions for Ethical Design

  • Only collect essential data (such as email), and make all other fields optional.
  • Label each input field with its intended purpose, and allow users to skip unnecessary fields.
  • Encrypt and isolate sensitive data (e.g., sexuality or income) and request explicit user consent before collection.
  • Offer subscription customization (e.g., topics and frequency) to give users more control.
  • Do not enable content targeting or personalization by default.
  • Provide tools for users to view, update, or delete their stored data at any time.
  • Publish regular transparency reports and encourage third-party ethical audits.
  • Involve diverse user voices early in the design process to ensure inclusive outcomes.

4. Design Philosophy and Broader Impact

Integrating ethical thinking into web design builds user trust and ensures legal compliance (e.g., GDPR). This shifts the digital experience from one of extraction and surveillance to one of empowerment and community. A privacy-conscious, respectful design helps redefine the website not just as a tool for outreach, but as a space for inclusive and transparent engagement.
